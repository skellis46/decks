

## C-DaRE strategic vision
### Field-leading dance research, embodied methods, wider impact

Note:
- vision for positioning C-DaRE over the next five years


---

### Dance is not only an object of study, but a way of producing methods, evidence and insight into human experience.

Note:
- remain rooted in dance research: somatic practices, dance and technology, inclusive cultural practices ...
- show more clearly how dance research contributes to wider challenge-led contexts; dance research is field with distinctive methods, knowledge and applications that are increasingly needed by other fields.

---

### Embodied methods are rigorous ways of researching movement, bodily experience, sensation, relation and practice as forms of evidence and knowledge.

Note:
- These methods give us routes into health, AI/digital technologies and creative cultures.

---

## Need for embodied methods

- how bodies experience health, pain, care and wellbeing
- how our embodied selves encounter AI, robotics and digital technologies
- how our sense of _being bodies_ shapes culture, identity and belonging

Note:
- each has Qs re human experience that body-centred research is ideal to address
- should be known for excellent research about dance AND what dance and embodied research makes knowable
- reveals key strategic tension

---

## The strategic tension

- protect the depth and specificity of dance research
- build credible routes to partnership, income, impact and public value
- position C-DaRE through translation without dilution

Note:
- ONE: dance research often works through long-term artistic, critical, archival, somatic and practice-based enquiry. That work should not be flattened into instrumental language.
- Nationally and internationally, C-DaRE as a Centre that can lead the field from within dance research, while also showing why dance research matters beyond itself.

---

## From strength to strategic position

- current work connects dance research to health, AI/digital technologies, identity and cultural memory.
- next phase is to organise and articulate more deliberately

Note:
- ONE: For example: Cisneros on Roma communities, identity and dance; Meehan on somatics and chronic pain; Whatley and Marsh on AI, robotics and disability; Crawley, Waelde, Stamp and (me) on heritage and cultural memory.
- SUM: five-years: turn our research depth into clearer strategic positioning: nationally and internationally within dance research, and more visibly in selected wider contexts where embodied methods matter.


---

## P1: sustained leadership in dance research

- excellence-led dance and practice research
- strong REF, PGR and ECR cultures
- visible national and international leadership
- collegial and ambitious research environment

Note:
- sustain visible leadership in journals, conferences, networks, knowledge exchange, examining and advisory roles
- continue to nourish conditions that already make Centre successful: research quality, collegiality, doctoral culture, practice research expertise and international credibility.

---

## P2: extend reach through embodied methods

- share tools to study bodies and experience
- new routes into health and wellbeing, AI/digital technologies, creative cultures and cultural resilience

Note:

- ONE: about what C-DaRE can offer other fields; they often recognise that bodies and experience matter, they do not always have the tools to study them. DR does.
- TWO: interdisciplinary ROUTE: health: pain, care, ageing, disability, etc are embodied; AI and digital technologies:  experience reshaped by interfaces, data and robotics; creative cultures: humans in motion carry memory, identity and cultural value.

---

## P3: make value legible and usable

- one clear Centre proposition
- concrete examples of C-DaRE work
- audience-specific language
- visibility beyond dance-specific contexts

Note:
- PRE: wider value is not always immediately understood outside dance research. By legible, I mean usable in bids, partnerships, REF narratives, PGR recruitment and internal decision-making.
- TWO: produce concrete examples for funders, university leadership, other disciplines, publics and partners;
- FOUR: increase visibility in selected professional, policy-facing and interdisciplinary contexts.
- SUM: already happening: goal to support bids, partnerships, internal visibility and external recognition.

---

## Five-year plan
### Year 1: articulate, map and align

- map research strengths, bids, partnerships, PGR activity and visibility
- produce the Centre proposition and 5–6 case studies
- identify 2–3 new partnerships

Note:
- ONE: map research strengths, bids, partnerships, PGR activity and visibility against the three principles
- TWO: already in process
- THREE: identify 2–3 new partnerships in health/wellbeing, AI/digital technologies, creative cultures/cultural heritage


---

## Five-year plan
### Year 3: consolidate, partner and test

- develop targeted bids and partner-led projects in the selected areas
- test language and case studies 
- build PGR/ECR activity around the same strategic routes

Note: 
- TWO: test language and case studies with funders, university leadership, partners and other disciplines; review and critique current REF cycle processes; test what works, learn from what does not, and adjust.

---
## Five-year plan
### Year 5: shape the field beyond dance

- C-DaRE is recognised as field-leading in dance research
- embodied, participatory and practice-based methods are sought by selected non-dance partners
- dance research has a clearer strategic role especially in health and AI/digital technologies


Note:
PRE: By “beyond dance”, I do not mean away from dance. I mean from dance: using dance research to shape how other fields understand bodies, movement, experience and practice.

---

## How we will know it is working

- research quality: strong REF, PGR and practice research outcomes
- income and partnership: more diverse bids, partners and funded routes
- recognition: invitations from selected non-dance contexts
- usability: proposition and case studies used in bids, REF narratives and advocacy

Note:
- quantitative: bid types and numbers, diverse income streams, PGR completions, publication outside field (inc trade), new partnerships, invitations. 
- qualitative: better recognition, stronger examples, clearer narratives, centre self-critique
